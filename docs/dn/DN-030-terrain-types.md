# DN-030: Terrain Types and Surface Ecology

**Status:** Draft
**Depends on:** DN-024 (terrain/wilderness), DN-023 (mining/geology)
**Related:** DN-026 (world discovery), raytrace.wgsl terrain_detail function

## Problem

The current 8 terrain types (grass, chalky, rocky, clay, gravel, peat, marsh, loam) are all soil composition variations. They look different but don't tell a STORY. The terrain should communicate what's underground, what happened here, and what can grow.

## New Terrain Types

### Mineral-Stained Soil (terrain type 8-10)

Surface indicators of underground mineral deposits. Generated from the same noise fields as mining veins (DN-023) so they spatially correlate.

**Iron-Stained** (type 8): Reddish-brown soil with rust-colored streaks. Found where iron oxide veins are close to the surface. A pleb with geology skill 4+ sees "Iron-stained soil — ore likely below."

**Copper-Stained** (type 9): Greenish-blue patina on the soil surface. Found where copper deposits are near-surface. Subtle — looks like discolored ground until identified.

**Flint-Bearing** (type 10): Pale chalky ground with dark flint nodules visible on the surface. Found where chalk/limestone contains flint. The first flint the player finds is probably from this terrain, not mining.

Visual: each has a base color (red-brown, green-grey, pale-dark) with per-pixel detail (streaks, nodules, patina). The terrain detail shader renders these as variations on the soil with mineral-colored highlights.

Gameplay: these are the "surface clues" from DN-023. A skilled geologist identifies them by walking near. An unskilled pleb sees "Discolored ground." Identified mineral soil is marked on the minimap. Building a mine on mineral-stained ground is more productive than random mining.

### Leaf Litter (terrain type 11)

Dark brown organic layer under dense tree canopy. Generated during worldgen wherever tree density exceeds a threshold.

Visual: dark brown with visible leaf shapes (noise-generated irregular patches in yellow-brown and dark brown). Occasional twig-like line details. Visually distinct from bare loam — leafy, organic texture.

Properties:
- High vegetation density (mushrooms, ground cover)
- Ashcap mushrooms spawn here after rain
- Rich soil (good composting, future farming bonus)
- Soft to dig (faster excavation)
- Indicates old-growth forest

Generated by checking tree density at each tile during worldgen. Dense forest areas (forest noise > 0.6) get leaf litter as their terrain type.

### Sand (terrain type 12)

Bright yellow-tan, low vegetation. Found near water features where streams deposit sediment, and in dry lowland areas.

Visual: bright warm tan with fine grain texture. Occasional small stone. Ripple patterns near water (wind-shaped). Sparse to no vegetation.

Properties:
- Very low vegetation density
- Glass-making raw material (sand + kiln → glass)
- Fast drainage (water doesn't pool)
- Easy to dig
- Found: along waterlines, in dry depressions, alluvial fans

### Volcanic Soil (terrain type 13, future)

Dark grey-black with occasional glassy fragments. Found near geothermal features (hearthstone/resonite deposits). Extremely fertile.

Needs further design — ties into alien geology and geothermal systems that don't exist yet. Reserved terrain type.

## Generation Rules

**Mineral-stained soil**: Use the same noise seeds as mining vein generation (DN-023 mining.rs). Where vein noise is high AND the tile is near-surface rock or has low elevation, set the terrain type to the corresponding mineral stain. This ensures mineral soil ACTUALLY indicates what's below.

**Leaf litter**: In `generate_terrain_with_params`, after the normal terrain type assignment, override tiles where tree density is high (forest noise > 0.55) with leaf litter type. This happens BEFORE vegetation placement, so the litter terrain type drives the "dark forest floor" visual even on tiles without trees.

**Sand**: Near water (seep_head > -0.5) AND low elevation AND low vegetation. Also in dry lowland depressions far from water (arid areas). Created by checking water table proximity and aridity.

## Rendering

Each type gets:
- A base color in `terrain_base_color()`
- Terrain-specific features in `terrain_detail()` with blended weights
- Appropriate vegetation density defaults in terrain generation
